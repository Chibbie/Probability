Addition Theorems or Theorms on total probability
Theorem 1
For mutually exclusive events A and B. Prob of A and B is given by individual probs of A and B
P (A U B) / P ( A +B ) = P(A) + P(B)
Theorem 2
For any number more than 2 events - K.

Theorem 3
Prob that either A or B occurs is by sum of individual probs less prob of simultaneous occurence
P (A U B) = P(A) + P(B) - (P N B)
Theorem 4
For any three events, prob that at least one of them occurs
P (A U B U C) = P(A) + P(B) + P(C) - P(A N B) - P(A N C) - P(B N C) + P (A N B N C)

Properties of expected value
E(k) = k for any constant k
Expectation of sum of two random variables is the sum of their expectations
Expectation of the product of a constant and a random variable is the product of the constant and the expectation of the random variable
Expectation of the product of two random variables is the product of the expectation of the two random variables, provided the two variables are independent
